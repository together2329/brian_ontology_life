# Global data-center public location baseline

This import is the reproducible location layer for Brian's global AI data-center
research. It queries every current OpenStreetMap object matching any of these tags:

- `telecom=data_center`
- `building=data_center`
- `industrial=data_centre`
- `industrial=data_center`

Run from the repository root:

```sh
python3 scripts/build_global_data_center_registry.py
```

The registry deliberately leaves GPU count, IT/utility power capacity, and power
equipment empty. Those fields are populated only in the separate deep-research
layer when a company filing, operator page, utility filing, permit, or other
traceable source supports the claim.

## Coverage boundary

"Every" in this file means every object returned by the documented OSM query at
the retrieval time. It does **not** mean every physical data center worldwide.
Private enterprise rooms, undisclosed hyperscale facilities, military sites, and
poorly mapped regions are systematically undercounted. A campus can also appear as
several building objects.

PeeringDB is used only as a non-republished cross-check because its acceptable-use
policy restricts bulk redistribution. On 2026-07-17 its public site reported 5,858
facilities, versus the independently queryable OSM tag baseline in this directory.

## Official operator registries

Operator-page registries in this directory are separate from the OSM-derived
database and retain their own source and disclosure boundaries:

- `cyrusone_official_location_pages.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_cyrusone_official_registry.py`.
- `qts_official_location_pages.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_qts_official_registry.py`.
- `microsoft_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_microsoft_official_region_registry.py`. This registry unions
  Microsoft's Datacenters Globe records with the Azure Learn public-cloud table
  and explicitly preserves source-only rows and upstream anomalies.
- `aws_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_aws_official_region_registry.py`. This registry reconciles the
  39-Region/123-AZ global marketing scope with the standard-account 34-Region/
  108-AZ-ID documentation scope and the GovCloud, China, and European Sovereign
  partitions. Marketing map IDs remain distinct from programmatic Region codes.
- `google_official_location_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_google_official_location_registry.py`. This registry reconciles
  Google's hidden map payload, visible list, FAQ roster, and selected detail
  pages. It preserves the Canelones lifecycle conflict and shows why the static
  30-location/11-country counters likely predate the 2025 Waltham Cross opening.
- `oracle_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_oracle_official_region_registry.py`. This registry reconciles
  Oracle's commercial, sovereign, and government marketing rows with the
  commercial OCI technical Region table. It preserves Hyderabad/Batam and Madrid
  code conflicts, separates 27 live commercial countries from the 28-country
  live-plus-planned headline scope, and treats 55 Availability Domains only as a
  physical-building lower bound against Oracle's separate 147-active claim.
- `meta_official_location_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_meta_official_location_registry.py`. This registry joins Meta's
  33 current directory cards to the 18 owned-online sites reported individually
  for calendar 2024. It keeps the remaining 15 labels unresolved rather than
  calling them offline, because leased facilities are aggregated and the current
  directory provides no lifecycle status field.
- `apple_official_location_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_apple_official_location_registry.py`. This registry joins Apple's
  named eight-site owned roster to six site-specific fiscal-2025 electricity rows
  and the one aggregate row covering its two Chinese sites. It keeps colocation,
  other third-party computing, and Google Cloud PCC facilities unlocated because
  Apple publishes energy or architecture boundaries but not physical rosters.
- `alibaba_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_alibaba_official_region_registry.py`. This registry reconciles
  the 32-public-Region/105-zone marketing scope with the technical guide's wider
  37-Region/130-zone public, Finance Cloud, and Gov Cloud scope. It separates two
  closing local Regions, one Preview Finance Region, and the Riyadh Partner Region;
  a zone can contain one or multiple data centers and is not one proven building.
- `tencent_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_tencent_official_region_registry.py`. This registry retains all
  22 current CVM Region rows and 62 AZ IDs, distinguishes 25 zones with different
  sales availability from lifecycle claims, and keeps 2025 owned/leased ESG
  metrics at portfolio scope. Tencent's AZ wording does not establish a building,
  address, ownership, MW, GPU, or site-economics ledger.
- `volcengine_official_region_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_volcengine_official_region_registry.py`. This registry preserves
  a nine-Region-ID union across three incompatible service scopes: the general
  guide's 7 Region rows/24 published AZs, Container Service's 6/20, and ECS's six
  endpoint rows. Five general-guide ZoneIDs that the reviewed official search
  snapshot did not render remain explicit residuals rather than inferred IDs.
  ByteDance's 2020 PUE, cooling, and renewable statements stay portfolio-scoped.
- `neocloud_disclosure_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_neocloud_disclosure_registry.py`. This registry covers the 12
  primary operators and five linked capacity hosts in the research roster while
  keeping active, contracted, secured, target, and pipeline MW separate. It also
  distinguishes installed, ordered, managed, customer-dedicated, and target GPU
  counts, and does not convert funding, backlog, ARR, or contract value into
  recognized revenue. No cross-company MW or GPU total is produced.
- `physical_operator_disclosure_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_physical_operator_disclosure_registry.py`. The 32-row crosswalk
  validates each major operator against a reviewed portfolio record and financial
  profile, carries official evidence URLs and source hashes, and links the deeper
  CyrusOne and QTS page-level registries. It requires one physical granularity,
  lifecycle, power denominator, and reporting boundary before any comparison.
- `nextdc_official_facility_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_nextdc_official_facility_registry.py`. The 29-label provider
  roster preserves lifecycle, card-MW, exact-address, site-equipment, GPU-ready,
  1H26 portfolio-measure, and OSM object boundaries instead of treating its
  1.82055-GW mixed-lifecycle checksum as live load.
- `flexential_official_facility_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_flexential_official_facility_registry.py`. The 41-card provider
  roster preserves the 40+/18-market/3M+ headline conflicts, a 4.985407-million-
  square-foot and 372.17-MW mixed-lifecycle checksum, five development cards,
  customer-owned GPU evidence, selected site equipment, 44 related OSM objects,
  and the separate 28-asset KBRA financial pool instead of treating any of these
  scopes as one operating-building or live-load ledger.
- `lumen_colocation_market_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_lumen_colocation_market_registry.py`. The registry transcribes
  all 120 city/state market rows from the 2025 provider PDF and separately
  reconciles the current 200+ location web headline, 175+ PDF headline, 25 OSM
  objects, six post-divestiture overseas labels, fleet-level power/cooling
  ranges, AI-network deal values, and the absence of site-level GPU inventory.
- `csquare_official_facility_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_csquare_official_facility_registry.py`. They reconcile 64 final-
  prospectus site rows, three planned closures, 36 current spec-sheet groups and
  22 OSM objects without treating 389 MW sellable, 392 MW contracted or 101%
  contracted-power-sold as actual metered utilization.
- `cdc_official_facility_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_cdc_official_facility_registry.py`. The 30 dated facility codes
  retain 18 operating, five construction and seven future-map states, the later
  unallocated 19/6 FY2026 portfolio counts, campus/region capacity boundaries,
  17 related OSM objects, location-page status conflicts, and undisclosed GPU and
  site-level electrical/cooling bills of materials.
- `global_data_center_operator_coverage_audit.jsonl`,
  `global_data_center_country_coverage_audit.jsonl`, and their summary are
  generated by `scripts/build_global_data_center_coverage_audit.py`. They retain
  every non-empty OSM operator label, country-level operator-tag gaps, reviewed
  profile routes, and a deterministic P1/P2 remaining-operator queue. The queue
  is research sequencing, not market share or a claim of global completeness.
- `power_cooling_supply_chain_registry.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_power_cooling_supply_chain_registry.py`. The 25-row crosswalk
  covers critical electrical systems, on-site generation, cooling, cable, and
  utilities. It retains company financial snapshots and campus links but does not
  convert group revenue, orders, backlog, MOUs, service agreements, equipment
  nameplate, or utility supply into data-center revenue or live critical IT load.
- `accelerator_disclosure_ledger.jsonl` and its summary are generated by
  `scripts/build_accelerator_disclosure_ledger.py`. Its 47 atomic claims point to
  exact reviewed evidence paths and separate physical GPUs or accelerator cards
  from delivered-chip flow, managed hardware, H100-equivalent compute, future
  targets, design accommodation, and supported architecture maxima. Every row is
  non-additive by default, and the summary deliberately publishes no fleet total.

These registries may mix operating, development, and future facilities. Published
capacity, area, investment, and equipment statements are not treated as current
energized, leased, utilized, or billed capacity unless a separate source supports
that lifecycle conversion. Microsoft Azure regions, availability zones, and
provider map coordinates are also not one-to-one physical buildings, exact street
addresses, or ownership records. Google location labels and detail pages can also
cover multiple physical sites, and Google map coordinates are retained only as
provider map anchors. Apple's eight owned labels are likewise sites or campuses,
not eight proven physical buildings; Apple explicitly describes multiple buildings
at Maiden, aggregates both Chinese sites in one energy row, and does not publish
the number of colocation or third-party PCC facilities. Alibaba Cloud defines a
zone as a physical area made of one or multiple scattered data centers, so its 130
published zone records are neither a building count nor a capacity total. Tencent
calls an AZ a physical data center, but its service table still omits building and
campus boundaries, addresses, ownership, capacities, lifecycle, and utilization.
Volcano Engine's Region/AZ counts vary by product, and its API-endpoint guide
explicitly says an endpoint is not necessarily the product's direct service
location; the nine-ID source union must therefore not be read as nine physical
Regions, campuses, or buildings. Neocloud service Regions, partner facilities,
GPU product ranges, financing announcements, and host leases have the same
scope problem: use the per-record lifecycle and ownership fields before comparing
  capacity or economics. The physical-operator crosswalk likewise does not rank
facility counts or sum capacity because buildings, facility IDs, campuses,
markets, design envelopes, live IT load, and utilized load are different objects.
The supply-chain crosswalk also requires one accounting basis, value pool,
commercial lifecycle, and site or segment allocation before comparing economics.
The accelerator ledger additionally requires one hardware unit, ownership state,
date, physicality class, lifecycle, site scope, and resolved overlap group.
